Tal Garfinkel is a research scientist and consultant with expertise in
security, virtualization, and operating systems.  He is the author of nearly 30
academic publications and eleven patents. His work has received the
distinguished paper and best paper awards at the Usenix Security Symposium, and
best paper award at Usenix Annual Technical Conference.

Dr. Garfinkel's has done foundational work in trusted computing (Terra),
software defined networking (SANE), and virtual machine introspection. In 2019
his paper introducing virtual machine introspection received the Network and
Distributed Security Symposium Test-of-Time award, for "[opening] the
floodgates on a tremendous amount of research and derivative tools" The paper
is the most highly cited NDSS paper (1751 citations) from the period 1995-2009.

In addition to his academic experience, Dr. Garfinkel worked for many years at
VMware, on research, product development, and security process improvements.
Dr. Garfinkel has served as a reviewer and program committee member for many
top academic conferences in systems and security, and was a co-founder of the
Usenix Workshop on Offensive Technology (WOOT), which is now in its 14th year.
He has also been a lecturer and invited speaker in both academic and industry
forums on topics in virtualization and security including Usenix Security and
Blackhat.

Dr. Garfinkel received his B.A. ('02) with Honors in Computer Science from the
University of California at Berkeley and his Ph.D.('10) from Stanford
University. He currently resides in the Northern California foothills with his
wife and two children. Apart from research, he enjoys rock climbing, hiking,
and backpacking in the nearby mountains.
