Tal Garfinkel is a Research Scientist at UC San Diego.  He is the author of
thirty-one academic publications and eleven patents. His work has received the
distinguished paper awards at Usenix Security and ASPLOS, and best paper award
at Usenix Security, and the Usenix Technical Conference.

Dr. Garfinkel's work has been foundational to trusted computing (Terra), software
defined networking (SANE), and introduced the technique of virtual machine
introspection (VMI).  In 2019, his paper on VMI recieved the NDSS "test of
time" award, for "[opening] the floodgates on a tremendous amount of research
and derivative tools" and was the most highly cited NDSS paper between 1995 and
2009.  Garfinkel was the co-founder and program co-chair of the Usenix Workshop
on Offensive Technology (WOOT).  He has also been a lecturer and invited
speaker in both academic and industry forums.

Dr. Garfinkel's recent work on Retrofitting Protection in the Firefox was
recognized with the 2022 IEEE Cybersecurity Practice Award, First place in the
2020 CSAW Applied Research Competition, and an Honerable Mention in the 2022
NSA Cybersecurity Research Paper Competition.

Dr. Garfinkel received his B.A. ('02) with Honors in Computer Science from the
University of California at Berkeley and his Ph.D.('10) from Stanford
University. And spent nearly a decade working for VMware during and after his
PhD.
