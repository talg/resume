Tal Garfinkel is an expert in topics at the intersection of software systems,
security, and computer architecture, with over a decade of industry experience.
Dr. Garfinkel is the author of 35 publications and 11 patents. His research has
been foundational to virtual machine based trusted computing (Terra,
Overshadow), software defined networking (SANE), and his work on data lifetime
lead to the development of cold-boot attacks.  His work introduced virtual
machine introspection, and received the 2019 Network and Distributed Systems
Security (NDSS) ``test of time'' award, for ``[opening] the floodgates on a
tremendous amount of research and derivative tools'' and was the most highly
cited NDSS paper between 1995 and 2009.  Dr. Garfinkel was the co-founder and
program co-chair of the Usenix Workshop on Offensive Technology (WOOT).  In the
past, he has recieved best paper awards at the Usenix Security and Usenix
Annual Technical confernces.  He recently received distinguished paper awards
at USENIX Security 2020 and ASPLOS 2023, the NSA Best Scientific Cybersecurity
Paper Competition (honorable mention) 2022, the IEEE Cybersecurity Award for
Practice in 2022, and an Intel Hardware Security Academic Award (Honorable
mention) in 2024. 

Dr. Garfinkel received his B.A. ('02) with Honors in Computer Science from the
University of California at Berkeley and his Ph.D.('10) from Stanford
University.
