Tal Garfinkel is an expert in software systems and security, with over a decade
of industry experience.  He has done foundational work on virtual machine based
trusted computing (Terra~, Overshadow), software defined networking (SANE), and
data lifetime that lead to the development of cold-boot attacks.  His work
introduced virtual machine introspection, and received the 2019 Network and
Distributed Systems Security (NDSS) ``test of time'' award, for ``[opening] the
floodgates on a tremendous amount of research and derivative tools'' and was
the most highly cited NDSS paper between 1995 and 2009.  Garfinkel was the
co-founder and program co-chair of the Usenix Workshop on Offensive Technology
(WOOT).  In the past he has recieved best paper awards at the Usenix Security
and Usenix Annual Technical confernces.  He recently received distinguished
paper and best awards at USENIX Security 2020 and ASPLOS 2023 and 2025, the NSA
Best Scientific Cybersecurity Paper Competition (honorable mention) 2022, the
IEEE Cybersecurity Award for Practice in 2022, and an Intel Hardware Security
Academic Award (Honorable mention) in 2024 .

Dr. Garfinkel received his B.A. ('02) with Honors in Computer Science from the
University of California at Berkeley and his Ph.D.('10) from Stanford
University.
